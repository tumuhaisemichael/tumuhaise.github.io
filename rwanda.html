National Parks in Rwanda

Rwanda offers unique wildlife experiences and breathtaking landscapes. Here are some of the most popular national parks:

Volcanoes National Park:

Volcanoes National Park, located in northwestern Rwanda, is renowned for its stunning volcanic scenery and as a sanctuary for mountain gorillas. Covering 160 square kilometers, it includes five of the eight volcanoes of the Virunga Mountains. The park is home to endangered mountain gorillas, golden monkeys, and a rich variety of bird species. Other wildlife includes bushbuck, black-fronted duiker, and several species of primates.

Nyungwe Forest National Park:

Nyungwe Forest National Park, located in southwestern Rwanda, is a pristine tropical rainforest spanning over 1,020 square kilometers. It is renowned for its rich biodiversity, scenic landscapes, and as a key water catchment area. Nyungwe is home to over 13 primate species, including chimpanzees and Rwenzori colobus monkeys. It boasts 300 bird species, such as the Great Blue Turaco, and numerous other wildlife including rare orchids and over 75 mammal species.

Akagera National Park:

Akagera National Park is located in northeastern Rwanda and covers approximately 1,122 square kilometers. It features a mix of savannah, woodlands, and wetland ecosystems, providing diverse habitats for wildlife. The park is known for its picturesque landscapes, including Lake Rwanyakazinga and the scenic hills of Rwanda. Akagera is home to a variety of species, including the Big Five (lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhino). It also boasts large populations of giraffes, zebras, and numerous antelope species. Bird enthusiasts can spot over 500 bird species, including the rare Shoebill stork.

Gishwati-Mukura National Park:

Gishwati-Mukura National Park is located in the western region of Rwanda, spanning approximately 34,000 hectares. It features lush montane forests, high-altitude wetlands, and scenic ridges. The park is home to various wildlife, including endangered golden monkeys, Rwenzori colobus monkeys, and a diverse range of bird species. It also supports mammals such as duikers and forest elephants.
